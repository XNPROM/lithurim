GFIW Thematic Idea: Battery supercycle boosts Alt Energy & EVs

Central Thesis:

  • Battery costs are falling sharply, according to analysis from UBS Research’s latest battery teardown enabling “triple parity” between EVs and ICE vehicles and triggering a clear inflection point for energy storage.
  • Battery energy storage system (BESS) economics are improving rapidly, driven by a forecast ~30% decline in system costs, double‑digit IRRs and accelerating data‑centre demand, positioning it as one of the highest-conviction growth stories in clean tech for the rest of the decade.
  • Energy security pressures are accelerating adoption and driving policy shifts towards electrification. In the US specifically, UBS Research believe BESS demand could triple to ~200 GWh by 2030E.
  • Lower battery cost and rising US onshore supply strengthen renewables-plus-storage's competitive edge benefitting US Alternative Energy. Chinese OEMs retain structural cost advantage, with BYD best positioned for global market share gains.


How to Express It: Investors with limited downside assumptions could use a funded call‑spread collar on Nextpower (NXT US) for targeted upside to U.S. renewables and storage or gain high‑conviction global battery and electrification exposure via BYD (1211 HK).

Thought Leaders’ Deep Dive:

Battery technology is reaching a clear inflection point across autos and power. UBS teardown analysis shows EVs approaching full “triple parity” with ICEs on cost, range and charging, shifting adoption from policy‑driven to consumer‑led. Cost leadership remains concentrated in China, where battery makers and OEMs retain a ~30–35% structural advantage, supporting ongoing global share gains despite rising trade barriers. These gains are accelerating BESS adoption, now the largest revenue pool after EVs with superior growth and margin potential.

Cost deflation is also rapidly reshaping grid‑scale storage, with accelerating AI data‑center power demand cementing BESS as essential. The US is leading deployment momentum, with US policy clarity, generous tax credits and surplus battery capacity enabling rapid scale‑up. BESS demand is set to roughly triple to ~200 GWh by 2030. UBS analysis sees onshore lithium-iron phosphate (LFP) battery costs well below consensus, driving a 30% drop in system costs. With policy support covering up to 50% of capex and double‑digit IRRs, momentum in US utility‑scale storage is strengthening. Renewables paired with storage now screen as the fastest, cheapest and most reliable source of dispatchable power. Interestingly, Korean battery makers are positioned to capture ~85% of US BESS market share by 2030E.
